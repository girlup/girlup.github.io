Legal & Fundraising

Maintaining our campaign finances, our legal issues, and our fundraising efforts requires a Legal & Fundraising department to work to ensure that the campaign can properly function. The accountants will maintain and assess our financial records and will also ensure that our campaign is complying to FEC guidelines for our campaign actions. Fundraising Managers will be the oversight heads of our specific methods and action of obtaining financial support. The primary task of these workers will be to communicate with our extensive network of campaign workers spread out over the country in addition to developing the specific strategies for campaign fundraising. The Legal Department Head will be paid to oversee management of this department.

Television Commercial (30 seconds)

A 30 second TV ad will be aired on popular national tv networks 40 times per day in the 4 days leading up the election and on Election Day. This concentration of advertisement for our campaign in the days before the election will hopefully help garner up a significant amount of support needed on Election Day.

Posters, Banners, Bumper Stickers, and Yard Signs

These physical advertisements expressing support for our campaign will help our widespread national support grow at the individual level. Spending a large supply on these items will help our supporters spread our campaign message and will help generate revenue for the campaign.

Poll

Our campaign will be working with a polling firm to conduct a 5 question survey in 13 different states: Colorado, Nevada, Florida, Iowa, New Hampshire, Wisconsin, Minnesota, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Virginia, North Carolina, California, and Texas. The reason why we are having our poll conducted in these states is because most of these states are somewhat equally Democratic and Republican with the exception of California and Texas. Getting data on voter ideas and feelings on various political issues can help our campaign adjust our strategies in those states in order to gain support. If our campaign gains strong support in these toss-up states, hopefully on Election Day, voters from these states can show up and help our campaign succeed. In addition, polling strongly partisan states like California and Texas can give our campaign data on the firm political beliefs from both sides of the political spectrum. This data will also help our campaign develop effective strategies and policies that can help win support over individuals in these states as well.

Radio Commercial (30 seconds)

A 30 second radio ad will be on air in the 3 months before Election Day. This ad will help our campaign reach millions of radio listeners that can increase support for our campaign.

Media

Ensuring that our campaign communicates to the public in the most effective and modern manner, employing social media liaisons will help our campaign efficiently communicate to numerous online media sites where they can communicate with users about the campaign, promote content from the campaign, and help organize support movements through the internet that can help increase our voter support for Election Day. A team of content producers will be paid to create digital content for our campaign which will help us with promotion. A Press Secretary will be a our official campaign liaison for communication with the press and a Communications Director will oversee the management of the campaign’s entire media/communications department actions.

Technology

Maintaining a resourceful database network for our campaign workers to access for various duties is important for the campaign to run effectively and efficiently. Database workers will ensure that our campaign has continuous access to data across our networks for our workers that are spread out over the country. A team of interactive media producers will be paid to develop applications and programs for worker usage within the campaign and for voter usage when browsing our outlets online. A Chief Technology Officer for our campaign will oversee the management of all ventures and activities made within the campaign’s technology department.

Scheduling

Though small, the department is tasked with ensuring that the campaign will be able to effectively make its presence known at public or private events, be it rallies, interviews, etc. Scheduling advisors will deal with managing events into the official schedule for the campaign on a national level. Internal coordinators, included in the field department, will work with these advisors to effectively manage the campaign’s schedule in all 50 states. A Deputy Scheduling Director will oversee management of the work done by the campaign scheduling advisors and the overall scheduling strategy.

Advisory

As time gets closer to Election Day, our campaign needs continuous development of our proposed political policies and our campaign strategies. A team of advisors will act as consultants for our campaign by proposing suggestions, ideas, and specific changes for our political policies and campaign strategies. A Communications Advisor will provide insight into our communications strategies and efforts while a Content & Issues Director will assist our campaign by managing the content that our campaign produces for the public. Our Foreign Policy Advisors, Domestic Policy Advisors, Economic Policy Advisors, and Energy Policy Advisors will work with our candidates and other top staff members to refine and develop our proposed policies. Finally, a campaign manager will work with the candidates to oversee the entire campaign.

Field

Having an efficient and effective field department is critical if our campaign wants to build an extensive network of support across the country for our campaign. Hiring individuals for numerous positions will help our campaign efficiently manage organization efforts in all 50 states. To oversee the management of all campaign operations in each state, a State Chairperson will be hired in each state to act as a representative leader for our state campaign efforts. In addition, a State Finance Chairperson will be hired in each state to oversee all campaign financial operations and management in that state. Our State Policy Directors in each state will work with our field coordinators and volunteers to assess our campaign strategies and proposed policies. Determining how a state reacts to a proposed policy from our campaign can help us improve on our proposed policies and help us develop new strategies to use in order to get more support from that state’s voters. A State Communications Director in each state will oversee all campaign communication efforts in that state. This includes management of media advertising, campaign events, and other ventures for campaign promotion. A State Deputy Volunteer Director in each state will oversee management of all volunteer efforts conducted in that state. In addition, Volunteer Coordinators in each state will be tasked with recruiting, scheduling, and retaining our campaign volunteers. State Scheduling Coordinators in each state will schedule campaign events in that state in coordination with our schedulers from our national campaign. Since our campaign aims to conduct extensive efforts in all 50 states, hiring Field Organizers in each state to organize various campaign efforts such as Get Out The Vote efforts will enable our campaign to reach as many voters as possible. Following the Field Organizers are the County Coordinators who will be tasked with conducting campaign efforts in the numerous counties across America. These efforts will include meetups, phonebanks, and other means of increasing campaign support. To oversee the management of our field operations on the nation level, our National Field Director will work with our top campaign officials to manage field efforts in conjunction with the efforts put forward by the national campaign.
